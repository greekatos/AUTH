MUSEUM

A MUSEUM FOR EVERYBODY

It is housed in a building constructed in 1962 according to the plans of the architect Patroklos Karatinos and is declared a preserved monument of the modern heritage. Its staff consists of scientific, technical and administrative personnel who work dedicatedly to offer quality services.

HISTORY

The history of the Archaeological Museum of Thessaloniki follows the course of the city's recent history. The Ephorate of Antiquities "in spite of the General Administration of Macedonia" was the first agency established, in November 1912, just fifteen days after the signing of the handover of the city to the Greek State.

HEADQUARTERS

The construction of the Archaeological Museum of Thessaloniki began in 1960, in the Square of H.A.N.Th., in the immediate vicinity of the site of the International Exhibition. The design of the building was done by the famous Greek architect Patroklos Karatinos, an important representative of the modern movement of architecture in Greece.

COLLECTIONS

The Museum Collections include numerous objects dating from prehistoric times to the end of antiquity. They come from excavations carried out throughout Macedonia since 1912, as well as from collections and traditions.

PEOPLE

The employees of the Museum are organized in departments according to their specialty. All together serve the same purpose. The administrative organization of the Museum is described in P.D. 4/2018 (Official Gazette 7/A/2018)

FRIENDS OF A.M.T.

The Association was founded in 1985 on the initiative of the late Ioulia Vokotopoulos, antiquities commissioner, director at the time of the Thessaloniki Antiquities Commission, which included the Archaeological Museum.
